1. Definition of the target language: folnuminqua

There is not one computationally transparent language. Just as in the world of the, their can be many. The SWiFT game makes use of a very simple computationally transparent language. The special thing about computationally transparent langauges is that you are free to introduce new words. Compared with a normal language (English) it could be seen as a language with a grammar, but with a minimum of predefined words. In a sense you extend the language (with new words) while you are using it.

The target language is defined as follows. A document in folnuminqua consists of a set of sentences, and sentences of a sequence of words and auxiliary symbols. Lets first explain the words, then the sentences and after that the documents.

1.1 Words

The words in Folminqua come in two flavours: constants and predicates.

1.1.1 Constants

A constant is a word that stands for a certain entity (which could be a person, a table, an armadillo, you name it). Examples of constants in Folnuminqua are:

  • c1

  • obama

  • oeiruij

  • pres_hujintao

You are free to choose any constant for a thing you want to refer to. You can choose the constant Obama to represent president Obama, but you could just as well have chosen c1. Although you are free to choose any constant to refer to the entity you have in mind, you must stick to your choice throughout the document. Each new occurrence of the same constant in the document, thus, refers to the same entity. On the other hand, you are free to connect different constants to the same entity, as long as not in conflict with the yet to exlpain equal or inequal statements.

1.1.2 Predicates

A predicate stands for a certain relation between entities. Examples of predicates are:

p

dlkfjiwjef

is_father_of

dk

likes

Just as with constants, you are free to choose any predicate for a relation you want to refer to. Again, you must stick to your choice throughout the document. Each predicate has a fixed number of entities it expresses a relation between. In Folnuminqua, simple as it is, this number must always be exactly 2.

1.2 Sentences

We will explain the sentences by giving examples.

Compare it to: lets call Obama p1 and Hu Jintao p2. Then it is true that p1 and p2 are both presidents.

The above statement expresses that the relation named p exists between c1 and c2 (and in that order, will be explained)

Sentence:

p(c1,c2)

Explanation:

we will call c1 and c2 constants, and p a predicate.

Each constant stands for a certain entity (which could be a person, a table, an armadillo, you name it) as defined by the player.

For example, c1 could stand for your best friend c2 for the child of your best friend.

The predicate stands for a certain relation between entities. The above statement expresses that the relation named p exists between c1 and c2 (and in that order, will be explained)

For example: p could be defined as follows: p(x,y) expresses the relation “x is a parent of y”. In that case, the statement above, p(c1,c2), expresses: c1 is parent of c2.

The order of constants is therefore important. p(c2,c1) and p(c1,c2) do not necessarily express the same information.

Different constants may refer to the same entity, as long as not in conflict with the equal or inequal statements (see below)

Constants and predicates are local names for entities and relations, in the sense that you can pick any you want. You don’t have to choose “Obama”. Compare it to: lets call Obama p1 and Hu Jintao p2. Then it is true that p1 and p2 are both presidents.

In this game, only two place predicates are allowed. I.e. p(c1) is not allowed, and neither is p(c1,c2,c3,c4)

equal(c1,c2,c3)

This expresses that the constants c1, c2 and c3 refer to the same entity.

Any nuymber of constants above or equal to 2 may be in the list. equal(c1) is incorrect, but equal(c1, c2,c3,c4,c5,c7) is correct.

The order of the is not of any importance. equal(c2,c3,c1) has the same meaning.

equal is not a predica

unequal(c1,c2,c3)

This expresses that the constants c1, c2, c3 refer to mutually distinct entities.

For the rest it has the same properties as equal.
